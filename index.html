About

Independent learner, system thinker, practical builder.

My passion for computing began in childhood and grew into more than eight years of self-directed learning in software engineering, system design, and computer systems.

Growing up in a rural area with limited internet access and few mentors taught me how to learn independently. I studied with borrowed textbooks, experimented with software, and explored electronics from Grade 11 and 12 materials before I had reliable online resources.

Programming is where I think most clearly. I enjoy solving logical problems, designing systems, automating repetitive tasks, and turning ideas into useful products.

Resilient Engineering

Improvised power generator

During the war between the TPLF and the Ethiopian government, I spent about six months in Kobo and nearby rural areas. With limited resources, I built a small generator using fan winding, a permanent magnet taken from a printer color-holder assembly, local tools, and available materials. It produced enough power to charge a laptop or light a bulb.
